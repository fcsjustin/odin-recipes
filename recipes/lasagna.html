Lasagna

Description:

Making lasagna is a rewarding process that starts with preparing a rich meat sauce, where ground beef or sausage is simmered with onions, garlic, and a blend of tomatoes and herbs until deeply flavorful. While the sauce cooks, you can also make a creamy ricotta mixture by blending it with egg, Parmesan cheese, and a bit of parsley, adding layers of texture and taste.

Assembling the lasagna involves layering sheets of pasta with the meat sauce, ricotta mixture, and a generous sprinkle of mozzarella cheese. The layers are repeated until the dish is full, then baked until bubbly and golden. The result is a hearty, comforting meal with gooey cheese and savory sauce, perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers.

Ingredients:

  • 1lb (450 g) ground beef or sausage (or a mix of both)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (6 oz) can tomato paste
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes

Steps:

  1. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the ground beef or sausage, breaking it up with a spoon, and cook until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan cheese, and chopped parsley. Mix until well combined.
  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
